Complexity Decoder

Lower bound vs upper bound

Lower bound means "no algorithm in this model can do better than this." Upper bound means "here exists an algorithm that can do at least this well."

Memory hook: Lower bound = floor. Upper bound = ceiling.

Worst, average, amortized

Worst-case is the guaranteed bad day. Average-case is the typical day. Amortized means one operation may spike, but a long run averages out nicely.

Memory hook: Worst = one storm. Amortized = climate over time.

Why sorting gets O(n log n)

Comparison sorting is boxed in by a lower bound of Omega(n log n). Merge sort and heap sort hit that upper bound, which is why they are "asymptotically optimal" among comparison sorts.

Memory hook: Decision tree height gives the floor.

When amortized matters most

Splay trees and Union-Find are classic amortized structures. One step may look ugly, but over many operations the average per operation is still very good.

Memory hook: Expensive clean-up, cheap long-term rhythm.
